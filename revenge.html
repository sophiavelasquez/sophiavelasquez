Three Cheers for Sweet Revenge

Three Cheers for Sweet Revenge is My Chemical Romance's second studio album. It was released on June 8, 2004 by Reprise Records. Several radio singles and MTV videos, including "I'm Not Okay (I Promise)", "Helena", and "The Ghost of You" were produced from this album.

Fun facts


  • This was the first album to include rhythm guitarist Frank Iero on all of the tracks.
  • This was the last album to feature drummer Matt Pelissier before he got replaced by Bob Bryar.
  • The album was certified platinum less than a year after it came out.

The storyline

"...the story of a man and a woman who are separated by death in a gunfight and he goes to hell only to realize by the devil telling him that she's still alive. The devil says he can be with her again if he brings the devil the souls of a thousand evil men and the man agrees to do it, and so the devil hands him a gun. That was the idea behind the concept, the record ended up being much more about loss and real life than anything, so I would say it's a good split."

Lead singer of My Chemical Romance: Gerard Way
